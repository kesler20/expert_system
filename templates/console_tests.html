Tests Hub

Within this section are compiled different tests that can be used to obtain data for design or powder characterisation

Powder properties include:

  • Flammability
  • Minimum Ignition Energy
  • Thermal Stability
  • Minimum Ignition Temperature
  • Burning Properties
  • Electrical Resistivity
  • Minimum Oxygen for Combustion
  • Explosion Characteristics
  • ST Classification

Hot surface test

These tests can be used to determine the operating range and the equipment that can be implemented, hot surface tests include:

  • layer ignition test
  • cloud ignition test

In order to determine sources of electrostatic sparks or to make the plant design inherently safer

you can carry out tests to find the Minimum ignition energy and Dust Explosion Tests such as:

  • vertical tube test
  • particle size analysis

In oreder to test how the fire spreads across a layer of dust you can perform a train fire test

To avoid dust clouds capable of exploding you can perform a minimum explosible concentrate test

To make your design robust you need to carry out explosion severity tests to predict the consequences of an explosion

  • 20 litre sphere Test
  • Kst test
  • Maximum explosion pressure sphere

In order to test how air impacts explosion risk you can perform a limiting oxygen concentration test (LOC)
